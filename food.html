Indian Food and Cuisine

Explore the diverse and delicious food culture of India.

North Indian Cuisine

Butter Chicken & Naan (Punjab)

A creamy tomato-based curry paired with tandoori-baked naan.

Rogan Josh (Kashmir)

A slow-cooked lamb dish infused with Kashmiri spices.

Dal Makhani (Punjab)

A slow-cooked black lentil dish with butter and cream.

Chole Bhature (Delhi/Punjab)

A spicy chickpea curry served with deep-fried bread.

South Indian Cuisine

Dosa & Sambar (Tamil Nadu/Karnataka)

A crispy rice crepe served with lentil soup and chutneys.

Hyderabadi Biryani (Telangana)

A fragrant rice dish layered with marinated meat and saffron.

Kerala Sadya (Kerala)

A traditional vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf.

Andhra Gongura Mutton (Andhra Pradesh)

A tangy mutton dish made with sorrel leaves.

East Indian Cuisine

Macher Jhol (West Bengal)

A Bengali-style fish curry cooked in mustard oil.

Rasgulla & Sandesh (Bengal/Odisha)

Soft, spongy desserts made from chhena (cheese curds).

Assamese Masor Tenga (Assam)

A tangy fish curry flavored with tomatoes and lemon.

West Indian Cuisine

Pav Bhaji (Maharashtra)

A buttery mashed vegetable curry served with toasted bread.

Goan Prawn Curry (Goa)

A coconut and tamarind-based prawn dish.

Dhokla (Gujarat)

A steamed fermented chickpea cake, light and fluffy.

Street Foods & Snacks

Pani Puri (Across India)

Crispy hollow puris filled with tangy, spicy water.

Vada Pav (Mumbai)

A spicy potato fritter inside a soft bun.

Kathi Rolls (Kolkata)

Spicy kebabs wrapped in flaky parathas.

Mirchi Bajji (Hyderabad)

Spicy deep-fried chili fritters.

Indian Sweets & Desserts

Gulab Jamun

Soft, fried milk dumplings soaked in sugar syrup.

Jalebi

Crispy, deep-fried spirals dipped in saffron syrup.

Mysore Pak

A rich, ghee-based sweet from Karnataka.

Modak

A steamed dumpling filled with jaggery and coconut, associated with Lord Ganesha.
