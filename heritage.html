Indian Heritage Sites

Qutub Minar (Delhi)

The tallest brick minaret in the world, built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in 1193. Represents the early Indo-Islamic architectural style.

Red Fort (Delhi)

A symbol of India's independence, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan. Features grand palaces, audience halls, and Mughal gardens.

Hampi (Karnataka)

The ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, known for its Virupaksha Temple, stone chariot, and intricate carvings.

Khajuraho Temples (Madhya Pradesh)

Built by the Chandela dynasty, famous for their erotic sculptures and detailed carvings. Divided into Hindu and Jain temple groups.

Mehrangarh Fort (Jodhpur, Rajasthan)

One of India's largest forts, built by Rao Jodha in the 15th century. Offers panoramic views of the "Blue City" of Jodhpur.

Amer Fort (Jaipur, Rajasthan)

Built with red sandstone and marble, featuring the Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace).

Mysore Palace (Karnataka)

The former residence of the Wodeyar dynasty, known for its stained glass windows and Dussehra celebrations.

Gwalior Fort (Madhya Pradesh)

A historic fortress with stunning blue-tiled walls and Jain rock carvings.

Ajanta and Ellora Caves (Maharashtra)

Buddhist rock-cut caves with murals, sculptures, and paintings dating back to the 2nd century BCE. Ellora has Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist caves, including the Kailasa Temple carved from a single rock.

Elephanta Caves (Maharashtra)

Located on Elephanta Island near Mumbai, these caves house massive rock-cut sculptures of Lord Shiva.

Badami Caves (Karnataka)

Ancient Hindu and Jain caves with beautiful Dravidian-style carvings.
